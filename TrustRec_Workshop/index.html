TrustRec Workshop

Nov 27, 2023

Ruihong Qiu, Associate Lecturer, Postdoctoral Research Fellow
University of Queensland, Australia

Bio:

Ruihong Qiu is a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the University of Queensland. He received his PhD in Computer Science at the University of Queensland in 2022 and BSc in Electrical Engineering at Beihang University in 2018. His research interests focus on theory and application of recommender systems. Ruihong’s research outcomes are published at SIGIR, ACM MM, WSDM, ICDM, CIKM, TOIS, etc. He has also served as a PC member or a reviewer of CIKM, WSDM, SIGIR, VLDB, TKDE and TOIS.
 

Title:

Graph Condensation for Continual Graph Learning

 
 

Thanh Tam Nguyen, Lecturer, Member of ACS
Griffith University, Australia

Bio:

Dr. Thanh Tam Nguyen is a lecturer at Griffith University. He earned his Master and PhD degrees from EPFL (Switzerland). His research interests include Databases and AI with special focus on Information Retrieval, Trust Management, Explainable AI for data networks and data streams. He published at SIGMOD, VLDB, SIGIR, ICDE, IJCAI, VLDBJ, TKDE, etc. He is a guest editor of IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics, an area chair of ACL and EMNLP, and a reviewer of AAAI, IJCAI, WWW, WSDM, VLDB, SIGIR, ICDE, KDD, EMNLP, CSUR, TKDE, JVLDB, TNNLS, Pattern Recognition, etc.

 

Title:

Towards Self-Adaptive LLM-based News Verification with Real-time Evidence

 
 

Junliang Yu, Postdoctoral Research Fellow
University of Queensland, Australia

Bio:

Junliang Yu is currently a postdoctoral research fellowing with the school of EECS at the University of Queensland. His research focuses on recommender systems, self-supervised learning, on-device machine learning and data-centric AI. His publications have garnered over 1,800 citations, of which six papers are identified as the most influential & ESI highly cited papers.
 

Title:

Towards Simple and Robust Graph Contrastive Learning for Recommendation

 
 

Shirui Pan, Professor, ARC Future Fellow
Griffith University, Australia

Bio:

Shirui Pan is a Professor and ARC Future Fellow with the School of ICT at Griffith University, Australia. His research focuses on graph neural networks, knowledge graphs, and large language models. He holds a Ph.D. in computer science from the University of Technology Sydney (UTS), and previously worked in the Faculty of IT at Monash University in Australia.
 

Title:

Towards Unifying Large Language Models and Knowledge Graphs
